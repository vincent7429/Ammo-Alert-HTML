Our Mission

Our mission at Ammo-Alert is to prevent tragic school shootings from occurring in the United States through the use of real-time gunshot detection. We use student computers to listen for bullets in the classroom setting, utilizing already existing hardware to minimize costs for administrators and educators. At Ammo-Alert, we prioritize privacy and do not store any student data or send audio to servers. Instead, we instantly alert appropriate subscribers when a firearm discharge is detected, ensuring students are prepared and able to respond. Created by students for students, Ammo-Alert is dedicated to making schools a safer place.

How It Works

1
Get It Installed
A representative from your school will install Ammo-Alert on your Windows© Computer 
2
Set up Contact Information
Open up the configuration (Settings Menu) screen and fill in Information such as a name, phone number, one emergency contact and the school address.
3
Let it run in the background
Ammo-Alert will run quietly in the background of the assigned computer, so you won't even know it's there until there are lives to be saved.

Easy to Use

Ammo-Alert is a school shooting detection app that is easy to use and requires no additional hardware purchases. It can be set up quickly in classroom settings and does not require a server or hub. Students can easily configure the app by providing some basic information about themselves. The app runs in the background to constantly listen for gunshots, ensuring student safety at all times. Additionally, the app has a user-friendly configuration screen that makes it simple for students to set up their own profiles. By using existing hardware and eliminating the need for additional equipment, Ammo-Alert reduces costs and setup time for schools.

Privacy is Important. 

More on Privacy Policy >
At Ammo-Alert, we understand the importance of privacy and the peace of mind it brings when it comes to school safety. That's why we take steps to ensure that your data is protected and secure. All saved data for is stored locally, not on cloud-based servers, and is only shared when a gunshot is detected. There is also an option to turn off the microphone at any time. We do not sell or share your data with any third parties, so you can trust that your privacy is our top priority.
